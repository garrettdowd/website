HyperloopTT

July 7, 2020

If you haven't had a chance to read the article on Glyde, now is your chance. My journey with HyperloopTT began while pitching Flexrail at Founders Forum, the premier conference for founders and CEOs in Europe.

At FF, I met Bibop Gresta, founder and former chairman of HyperloopTT. He charismatically criticized the rail industry while painting a picture of how Flexrail could pivot to a new industry. Somehow my work with HyperloopTT developed quickly in the following months and Flexrail began to pivot into Glyde. 

I started on a crypto-currency project using Glyde as a blueprint to build a utility strategy for a new crypto-currency. I delivered a whitepaper to executive leadership and worked with them directly on strategy until the project was cancelled. After the crypto-currency project I continued to collaborate with exec leadership across a range of topics.

Overall, I worked with HyperloopTT across 4 continents developing strategy and working with stakeholders. The pictures above are from a few of those experiences.

However, it is important to point out that HyperloopTT is not an average startup. Harvard Business School developed a case study around HyperloopTT's business model. To summarize, Hyperloop has only a handful of full-time employees and instead utilizes a large group (800+) of contributors and partner companies to achieve business goals. These contributors and partners receive future equity in exchange for their work. I was one of these contributors and my involvement was part-time.
