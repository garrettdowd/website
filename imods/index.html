iMods

July 7, 2020

A great smartphone makes tasks simpler and easier. Apple is famous for creating simple devices that make our lives easier, but with 300+ million users is a one-size-fits-all approach really that simple anymore? - iMods Pitch 2014

Intro

Apple has historically locked down the iOS operating system and the App Store to prevent users and developers from modifying/adding core features and UI/UX. This is especially true when compared to a mobile OS like Android. However, this did not stop tech nerds/geniuses/gods from "hacking" iOS, using techniques like code injection, to overcome Apple imposed limitations.

In fact, many features that we could not live without in current versions of iOS like cellular hotspot, mission control, notification center, in-app messaging, and more were all originally created and released by these tech/design geniuses within the so-called jailbreak community. In another manner of saying, Apple mimicked (ripped-off) many of the features and UI/UX enhancements developed by the jailbreak community.

Even Apple founder and tech god Steve Wozniak was a fan,

I have always given my support to the jailbreak community because they remind me of myself when I met Steve Jobs and how we were, then and for the years leading to Apple. -Steve Wozniak

iMods was born from this "jailbreak" community back in 2014. It was an ambitious attempt to modularize and appify iOS using jailbreak software as the building blocks. iMods was an attempt to solve two major problems with Cydia, the existing jailbreak marketplace at the time.

  • Piracy. Cydia enabled rampant piracy, hindering talented developers efforts. 
  • Usability. Understanding how to jailbreak an iOS device and what software was safe to install was a high barrier to entry. 

We created an App Store like marketplace that enabled an average iOS user to easily install vetted enhancements on their iOS devices.

Again, in the words of Steve Wozniak,

 I think it's a great thing...to make the software work exactly the way they need it to. That kind of hacking is the center of my heart, that's who I am. - Steve Wozniak

This was iMods.
